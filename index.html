Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about cat food? We've got answers.

How do you rate cat food?

We evaluate cat food based on ingredient quality, nutritional value, brand reputation, customer reviews, and value for money. Our ratings are based on comprehensive research and real-world feedback from cat owners.

What makes a cat food "highly rated"?

Highly rated cat foods typically have high-quality protein sources as the first ingredient, minimal fillers, no artificial preservatives, and positive feedback from veterinarians and cat owners. They also provide complete and balanced nutrition for your cat's life stage.

Should I switch my cat's food?

If your cat is healthy and thriving on their current food, there's no need to switch. However, if you notice digestive issues, skin problems, or your cat seems uninterested, it might be time to try a different formula. Always transition gradually over 7-10 days.

How much should I feed my cat?

Feeding amounts vary based on your cat's age, weight, activity level, and the specific food formula. Check the packaging for recommended serving sizes, and consult with your veterinarian for personalized feeding guidelines.

Are grain-free foods better for cats?

Grain-free foods can be beneficial for cats with grain allergies or sensitivities. However, most cats can digest grains without issues. The most important factor is that the food provides complete nutrition with high-quality protein sources.

Can I feed my cat both wet and dry food?

Yes! Many veterinarians recommend a combination of wet and dry food. Wet food helps with hydration, while dry food can help maintain dental health. The key is ensuring your cat receives the right total daily calories.
